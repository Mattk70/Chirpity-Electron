Model options
Use whitelist Enabled by default. This option limits the species detections shown to those recorded at UK Nocmig sites submitting data to Trektellen.

If disabled, the full list of potential detections is used. This includes UK vagrants as well as those birds not known to call or fly at night. Non-bird detections will also appear.

This full list comprises 306 bird species and the following non-bird classes:
  • Ambient noise: wind, rain, pops and bangs. Things that go bump in the night...
  • Animals: cats, dogs, bats, etc.
  • Human: voices, whistles, etc.
  • Vehicles: Wheel and engine noise of cars, trucks, trains and aircraft. Also sirens and alarms (including domestic burglar alarms) and horns.

If you wish to see the full list of species Chirpity Nocmig was trained on, it is contained in the feedback prompt offered when giving feedback on an incorrect prediction.

Disabling the whitelist is not recommended for general use. It will lead to an increase in false positive detections, and clutter the results table with irrelevant detections.
Spectrogram options
Enable Whether to display the Spectrogram view. Will reduce the computer RAM required for larger files, at the cost of significantly reduced functionality. However, results will be displayed and a label file may be exported for use in Audacity.
Timeline Whether to display a timeline above the spectrogram. The timeline has two modes.
  • Time Elapsed displays the time from the beginning of the file.
  • Time of Day uses the file timestamp to calculate the actual time of the calls detected. If a file does not have a timestamp reflecting the time of the recording, this will give a spurious reading.
Colour Displays the spectrogram in colour
Grayscale Displays the spectrogram in black and white.
